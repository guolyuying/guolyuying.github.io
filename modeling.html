NPR Shader

The graphic, stylized shading and rim lighting in the final render are generated by this Emission shader network in Blender.

The screenshot is an example of her pink suit's shader. Other parts of the model have slightly different versions of this shader to achieve the specific look I need.

For example, the skin shader has the warm rim light Mix Color node set to a darker color to achieve that warm orange rim instead of a reflective rim light, since skin behaves differently from other surfaces. Her eyeballs don't have rim lighting in the shader at all because I don't want a bright blue spot dancing on her eyeballs.

NPR Outline

The stylized outlines are generated by a simple Emission shader and a Solidify modifier in Blender, commonly known as "The Inverted Hull Method."

A few important checkboxes and numbers that are crucial to the final look are marked in the screenshot above.

Based on "Goodbye Summer", a Saturday Evening Post cover illustration by J.C. Leyendecker, published in 1934
