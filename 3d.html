Artist's Journal

A previous 2D artist, I will focus on 3D art & animation in graduate school and make a career transition.

3D has broadened my creative skillset and has allowed me to "draw" in a way I never imagined before.

I used to be afraid of 3D while focusing on drawing and painting in college. There's no "mistake" in drawing - I loved being able to try everything without technically failing. The only 3D software I learned back then was SketchUp - to assist perspective drawing. However, having spent almost a year exploring Blender after graduation, I started to enjoy the problem-solving nature of 3D art. This craft challenged me to embrace the trials and errors of technical artistry, and has helped me grow into a more patient and persistent person.

I hope to update my work here as I progress further.

P.S. Seriously, I respect every 3D artist out there, whether beginner or experienced master. This craft is not something easy to pick up or keep pushing forward with. Y'all are winners :)
